📚 Vocabulary Reference

toll great, fantastic ↔ schlecht ⚠️ False friend: English 'toll' = a fee. German toll = great/fantastic!
normal normal, ordinary ↔ komisch / abnormal True cognate: English 'normal', Spanish 'normal' — same meaning in all three.
komisch strange, odd (also: funny/comical) ↔ normal ⚠️ False friend: Spanish 'cómico' / English 'comical' suggest humour. German komisch primarily means strange/odd.
blöd stupid, annoying, disagreeable ↔ toll / gut Colloquial adjective. Very common in everyday German speech.
interessant interesting ↔ langweilig True cognate: English 'interesting', Spanish 'interesante'. Note: German ending -ant vs. English -ing.
lustig funny, amusing, cheerful ↔ langweilig / traurig ⚠️ False friend: English 'lusty' means vigorous/lively — not the same. German lustig = funny/amusing.

✅ Solutions

1. toll
toll means great/fantastic in German. Beware: English 'toll' is a false friend (it means a fee).
2. komisch
komisch means strange/odd in German. False friend: Spanish cómico / English comical suggest humour.
3. lustig
lustig means funny/amusing. False friend: English 'lusty' has a different meaning.
4. interessant
interessant is a true cognate with English 'interesting' and Spanish 'interesante'. Note -ant ending in German.
5. normal
normal is a true cognate in German, English, and Spanish — same meaning in all three languages.
6. blöd
blöd means stupid, annoying, or disagreeable. It is a colloquial adjective common in everyday German speech.
7. interessant / lustig
Two adjectives used predicatively (after ist): no adjective endings needed in this position.
8. toll / blöd
Both adjectives are used predicatively after finden. No inflection needed after finden es ___.
9. komisch
komisch used predicatively (after ist) in a dass-clause. No ending change needed here.
10. tolles
toll before a neuter noun (das Konzert) in accusative takes -es ending: ein tolles Konzert.
11. a) toll
toll = great/fantastic. normal = ordinary. komisch = strange. Context: expressing enthusiasm.
12. b) lustig
lustig = funny/amusing — fitting someone who always does funny things.
13. b) interessant
interessant (interesting) is the most direct antonym of langweilig (boring) in this context.
14. b) lustig
lustig = funny; the context of making someone laugh points to lustig specifically.
15. b) tolles
das Hobby — neuter; after ein (indefinite article) in nominative: ein tolles Hobby (-es ending).
16. b) blöd
blöd = disagreeable/annoying — fits the context of not wanting to go to work.
17. Das Konzert ist toll! Ich möchte wiederkommen.
toll used predicatively after ist — no ending change. möchte = would like to.
18. Ist das normal? Er tanzt nie.
normal used predicatively. nie (never) from Lektion 7. tanzt (er-form of tanzen).
19. Der Film ist sehr lustig und interessant.
Both adjectives used predicatively after ist — no endings needed. sehr (very) intensifies lustig.
20. Es ist komisch, dass er singen kann, aber nie ins Konzert geht.
komisch predicatively. dass-clause: verb (kann, geht) goes to end. ins Konzert gehen = to go to a concert.
21. Er findet Kickboxen interessant, aber ich finde es ein bisschen blöd.
finden + adjective = to find something (adj). ein bisschen = a little bit. Both adjectives are predicative.
22. Der DJ war wirklich lustig — wir haben den ganzen Abend getanzt und gelacht.
lustig predicatively after war. tanzen → hat getanzt; lachen → hat gelacht. den ganzen Abend = all evening (accusative).
23. Das Konzert war sehr toll und interessant.
When adjectives are used predicatively (after war), they take no ending. interessantes is wrong — it should be interessant.
24. Er ist ein komischer Mann.
komisch before a masculine noun (der Mann) after ein takes -er ending: ein komischer Mann.
25. Ich finde das Hobby toll, aber es ist ein bisschen langweilig und blöd.
Predicative adjectives after ist take no ending: blödes should be blöd.
26. Correct! Das ist komisch! Er kann nicht normal tanzen.
This sentence is actually correct. komisch (strange) used predicatively after ist — no ending. normal used as an adverb before tanzen — no ending either.
27. Der Film war lustig, aber die Geschichte war ein bisschen blöd.
lustig used predicatively after war — no ending (-e ending is wrong here). blöd is correct.
28. Ich finde sein Hobby sehr interessant — er malt und fotografiert.
After finden + object, the adjective is predicative: interessantes should be interessant (no ending).
29. langweilig
langweilig (boring) is the antonym of interessant (interesting).
30. schlecht
schlecht (bad) is the direct antonym of toll (great). Note: blöd can also function as an antonym in informal speech.
31. komisch / abnormal
komisch (strange/odd) or abnormal are the antonyms of normal.
32. Ich finde das sehr lustig.
finden + accusative object (das) + predicative adjective (lustig — no ending).
33. Sie findet Tanzen interessant und lustig.
Tanzen (infinitive as noun) is the object. Both adjectives predicative after findet — no ending.
34. interessante
interessant before feminine noun (die Musik) after keine: keine interessante Musik (-e ending).
35. b) blöd
blöd = disagreeable/annoying — fits rainy, cold weather. toll = great. lustig = funny.
36. interessant
interessant predicatively after sind (plural). No ending needed in predicative position.
37. Das ist eine tolle Idee! Das Konzert klingt lustig.
lustig does not have an umlaut. lüstig is not a German word — the correct spelling is lustig.
38. Es ist komisch, dass er Schwimmen langweilig und gleichzeitig interessant findet.
komisch predicatively. dass-clause: findet goes to end. gleichzeitig = at the same time.
39. lustig
lustig used as an adverb (funny way of singing). No ending change when used as adverb.
40. c) toll
Context (laughing a lot, dancing well) indicates a great time — toll. blöd = bad/annoying. komisch = strange.
41. Kochen ist normal für ihn — er kocht jeden Tag.
Kochen (infinitive as noun, neuter) is subject. normal predicatively after ist. jeden Tag = every day (accusative).
42. komisch / langweilig
komisch = strange (predicatively after finde es). langweilig = boring (predicatively after ist).
