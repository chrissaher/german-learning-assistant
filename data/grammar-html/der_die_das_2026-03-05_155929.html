📖 Strategy & Patterns

How to tackle gender in A1 vocabulary

Since you already understand what der/die/das mean, the challenge is knowing which one applies to each noun. There is no single perfect rule — but two tools help:

  1. Pattern recognition: certain noun endings reliably predict gender. Learn them and make educated guesses for new words.
  2. Memorize as a unit: always learn the article with the noun — never Hotel alone, always das Hotel. Treat the article as part of the word.

At A1 level, you do not need every German noun — just the ~100–150 words in Momente A1 and travel contexts. This session drills the most important ones.

Reliable ending patterns — your best guessing tool

EndingGenderReliabilityA1 examples
-ung die (f) ⭐⭐⭐ very high die Wohnung, die Zeitung, die Reservierung, die Rechnung
-heit / -keit die (f) ⭐⭐⭐ very high die Freiheit, die Möglichkeit
-tion / -sion die (f) ⭐⭐⭐ very high die Station, die Information, die Lektion
-e (places & abstracts) die (f) ⭐⭐ high die Straße, die Apotheke, die Küche, die Schule
-er (agent nouns) der (m) ⭐⭐ high der Kellner, der Lehrer, der Bäcker
Days / months / seasons der (m) ⭐⭐⭐ always der Montag, der März, der Sommer
-chen / -lein (diminutives) das (n) ⭐⭐⭐ always das Mädchen, das Brötchen
-um (Latin/Greek) das (n) ⭐⭐ high das Museum, das Datum
-ment das (n) ⭐⭐⭐ very high das Dokument, das Instrument
Infinitive as noun das (n) ⭐⭐⭐ always das Essen, das Frühstück
Modern borrowed words das (n) ⭐ moderate das Ticket, das Taxi, das Handy, das Hotel

⚠️ Watch out: der Junge (ends in -e but masculine) · das Café (ends in -é but neuter) · die Butter (ends in -er but feminine). Exceptions are rare at A1.

🌍 Transfer Notes

  • Spanish gender rarely matches German gender — don't use Spanish as a guide. el sol (m) → die Sonne (f); la ciudad (f) → die Stadt (f) ✓ — some match, many don't.
  • English gives you zero information about German gender — 'the' never changes. Memorizing the article is the only way.
  • The -ung ending → die is your single most reliable pattern at A1. If you see an unfamiliar -ung noun, guess die with confidence.
  • Borrowed words from English (Ticket, Taxi, Handy, Internet) are often neuter. When in doubt about a modern borrowed word, guess das.
🔑 Key Takeaway: Learn every A1 noun as a fixed pair (article + noun), use -ung → die and -chen/-lein → das for unknowns, and default to das for modern borrowed words.

✏️ Examples

Simple Basic article identification

1. Das Hotel ist sehr schön.
→ The hotel is very nice.
das Hotel — neuter; borrowed word, das is common for modern borrowed nouns.
2. Der Bahnhof ist groß.
→ The train station is big.
der Bahnhof — masculine; must-memorize noun, no reliable ending pattern.
3. Die Apotheke ist rechts.
→ The pharmacy is on the right.
die Apotheke — feminine; -e ending on a place noun → usually die.

Medium Multiple nouns per sentence

4. Die Wohnung hat zwei Zimmer.
→ The flat has two rooms.
die Wohnung (feminine, -ung ending) + das Zimmer (neuter, must-memorize).
5. Der Kaffee kostet zwei Euro.
→ The coffee costs two euros.
der Kaffee — masculine; must-memorize, the -ee ending is not a reliable pattern.
6. Die Reservierung ist für morgen Abend.
→ The reservation is for tomorrow evening.
die Reservierung — feminine; -ung ending → always die.

Complex Multiple genders, real contexts

7. Der Zug nach München fährt um acht Uhr ab.
→ The train to Munich departs at eight o'clock.
der Zug (masculine, must-memorize) · die Uhr (feminine, must-memorize).
8. Das Ticket kostet zwanzig Euro und der Pass ist im Koffer.
→ The ticket costs twenty euros and the passport is in the suitcase.
das Ticket (neuter, borrowed) · der Pass (masculine) · der Koffer (masculine, -er pattern).
9. Die Rechnung, bitte — das Restaurant hat ein gutes Frühstück.
→ The bill, please — the restaurant has a good breakfast.
die Rechnung (feminine, -ung) · das Restaurant (neuter, borrowed) · das Frühstück (neuter, from infinitive).
10. Das Museum ist in der Stadtmitte, neben der Kirche.
→ The museum is in the city centre, next to the church.
das Museum (neuter, -um ending) · die Kirche (feminine, -e place noun).
11. Der Kellner bringt das Bier und die Rechnung.
→ The waiter brings the beer and the bill.
der Kellner (masculine, -er agent noun) · das Bier (neuter, must-memorize) · die Rechnung (feminine, -ung).
