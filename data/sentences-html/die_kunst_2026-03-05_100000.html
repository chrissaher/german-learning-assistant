Simple 3 sentences

1.Simple
Die Kunst ist schön.
→ Art is beautiful.
📝 Die Kunst is feminine nominative (subject); predicate adjective schön takes no ending after ist.
2.Simple
Ich liebe die Kunst.
→ I love art.
📝 Accusative of feminine die Kunst is identical to nominative — the article does not change for feminine nouns.
3.Simple
Das ist eine Kunst.
→ That is an art.
📝 Eine Kunst uses the feminine indefinite article; this construction defines or categorizes something.

Medium 3 sentences

4.Medium
Die Kunst im Museum ist sehr interessant.
→ The art in the museum is very interesting.
📝 Im = in + dem (dative contraction); die Kunst remains nominative as subject of ist.
5.Medium
Wir sehen die Kunst in der Galerie.
→ We see the art in the gallery.
📝 Die Kunst is accusative direct object; in der Galerie is dative (in + der, feminine dative article).
6.Medium
Die Künste in Wien sind sehr bekannt.
→ The arts in Vienna are very well known.
📝 Die Künste is the nominative plural form; plural subjects take sind (not ist); Wien takes no article.

Complex 4 sentences

7.Complex
Ich habe die Kunst in der Galerie sehr schön gefunden.
→ I found the art in the gallery very beautiful.
📝 Perfekt: habe + gefunden (past participle of finden at the end); die Kunst is accusative object.
8.Complex
Du kannst die Kunst im Museum jeden Tag sehen.
→ You can see the art in the museum every day.
📝 Modal verb kannst sends the infinitive sehen to the end; jeden Tag is an accusative time expression.
9.Complex
Die Künste in dieser Stadt sind alt und sehr schön.
→ The arts in this city are old and very beautiful.
📝 Dieser Stadt is dative feminine (in + dieser); two predicate adjectives joined by und, both undeclined.
10.Complex
Wir haben viele Künste im Museum gesehen und viel gelernt.
→ We saw many arts at the museum and learned a lot.
📝 Perfekt with two past participles (gesehen, gelernt) sharing the auxiliary haben; viele Künste is accusative plural.
