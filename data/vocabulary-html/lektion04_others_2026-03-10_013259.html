📚 Vocabulary Reference

zu Hause phrase at home Fixed expression: static location. Contrast: nach Hause = going home (movement).
oh interjection oh Surprise or admiration: Oh ja! (Oh yes!) / Oh nein! (Oh no!)
mal adverb just / once (softening particle) Softens commands: Schau mal! (Just look!). Very common in spoken German — no direct Spanish/English translation.
da adverb there / over there Location: Schau mal, da! (Just look, over there!). Also used as 'here' in some contexts.
wie viel phrase how much Used for price questions: Wie viel kostet das? Two-word expression. Spanish: ¿cuánto?
denn adverb then / (modal particle in questions) Makes questions more conversational and curious: Wie viel kostet er denn? (How much does it cost, then?). No direct Spanish equivalent.
nur adverb only / just Limits or restricts: Das kostet nur 10 Euro. Spanish: solo/solamente.
wirklich adverb really / truly Intensifier: Das ist wirklich schön! (That is really beautiful!). Spanish: verdaderamente / de verdad.
zu adverb too (excessively) Before adjectives: zu teuer (too expensive), zu klein (too small). Different from preposition 'zu' (to) — context determines meaning.
so adverb so / such Intensifier: Das Bild ist so schön! (The picture is so beautiful!). Spanish: tan. From the Lektion 4 title!
